#### INFORMATION ABOUT COMP 2156 DEVOPS COURSE
=================================================

The COMP 2156 DevOps course at George Brown College is a contemporary
offering designed to introduce students to the fundamentals and best
practices of Development Operations (DevOps). This course is part of the
collegeâ€™s commitment to providing cutting-edge, industry-relevant education,
aimed at bridging the gap between software development and IT operations.
It focuses on the automation and integration processes that improve the
collaboration between development and IT operations teams, ensuring faster
and more efficient delivery of applications and services.

Key DevOps Principles Covered:
- Continuous integration (CI)
- Continuous delivery (CD)
- Automated testing
- Infrastructure as code (IaC)

Throughout the course, students will delve into these key DevOps principles,
structured to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical skills, with
hands-on projects and exercises that simulate real-world scenarios. Students
will learn to use popular DevOps tools and technologies, such as Jenkins,
Docker, Kubernetes, Ansible, and Git, among others.

This course is suitable for students pursuing careers in software development,
IT operations, or any related field that benefits from the DevOps culture of
enhanced collaboration and efficiency. By the end of the course, students will
have a solid understanding of how to implement DevOps practices in a
professional environment, aiming to improve the speed, quality, and reliability
of software development and deployment processes.
