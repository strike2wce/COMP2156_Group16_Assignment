# Information About COMP 2156 DevOps Course

The **COMP 2156 DevOps** course at George Brown College is a contemporary offering designed to introduce students to the fundamentals and best practices of Development Operations (**DevOps**). This course is part of the collegeâ€™s commitment to providing cutting-edge, industry-relevant education, aimed at bridging the gap between software development and IT operations. It focuses on the automation and integration processes that improve the collaboration between development and IT operations teams, ensuring faster and more efficient delivery of applications and services.

Throughout the course, students will delve into key DevOps principles, including **continuous integration (CI)**, **continuous delivery (CD)**, automated testing, and **infrastructure as code (IaC)**. The curriculum is structured to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical skills, with hands-on projects and exercises that simulate real-world scenarios. Students will learn to use popular DevOps tools and technologies, such as **Jenkins**, **Docker**, **Kubernetes**, **Ansible**, and **Git**, among others.

This course is suitable for students pursuing careers in software development, IT operations, or any related field that benefits from the DevOps culture of enhanced collaboration and efficiency. By the end of the course, students will have a solid understanding of how to implement DevOps practices in a professional environment, aiming to improve the speed, quality, and reliability of software development and deployment processes.
